Executive Summary

School Name, its Principal/Headmaster/Headmistress and its staff owe a duty to take care of students whilst they are involved in School/College activities or are present for the purposes of a School/College activity.

This duty of care is non-delegable meaning that it cannot be assigned to another party.

This duty of care requires taking reasonable measures, regarding all the circumstances, to protect students from risks of harm and injury that should have been reasonably foreseen.

It also extends to taking reasonable care that any student (and other person) on the School/College premises will not be injured or damaged by reason of the state of the premises or of things done, or omitted to be done, in relation to the premises.

The standard of care required is that of a “reasonable” teacher. This means that the duty of care owed is the duty one would expect from a hypothetical teacher with normal skills and attributes exercising their professional judgment.

The individual circumstances of students, such as age, medical conditions and behavioural characteristics will determine what constitutes reasonable care.

Our Student Duty of Care policies are categorised as follows:

  • General Principles (including Incident Management)

  • Student Welfare and General Safety (including Supervision)

  • Student Health (including Anaphylaxis and Asthma)

  • Student Behaviour (including Bullying and Cyber Safety)

  • Extracurricular Activities (including Excursions)

At School Name we manage student safety through a combination of policies, staff learning, assurance and management reporting.
