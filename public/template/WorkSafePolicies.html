Broken Glass
Broken Glass Glass is fragile and subject to breakage. When glass breaks it has the potential to cause injury, not only to those who are tasked with the responsibility of cleaning up the breakage, but also to others who may be cut by broken glass if it is not cleaned up and disposed of effectively.
School Name's Policy School Name is committed to providing a safe working environment for all our students, workers, visitors and guests. It is our policy that where there is a high risk of glass breakage that may cause injury, plastic rather than glass objects be used where practical. In the event of glass breakage, the affected area must be immediately isolated, and the glass disposed of, following the guidelines outlined in this Policy.
Safe Work Practices We have developed the following safe work practices for managing broken glass.
Preventative Action Where there is a high risk of glass breakage that may cause injury (for example at large scale School/College social events), where practical, plastic rather than glass should be used in order to reduce the possibility of broken glass injuries.
Adequate Footwear Students, workers, visitors and guests attending the School/College premises are required to wear adequate footwear to minimise the risk of injury from broken glass. In certain higher risk situations, such as when working in science laboratories, covered footwear must always be worn.
Isolating the Hazard In the event of glass breakage, the surrounding area must be immediately isolated by letting everyone in the vicinity know of the danger and preventing others from entering the danger area. The method of isolation will depend on the situation and the perceived risk of injury. This may involve asking bystanders to create a “human barrier” to the danger area while others undertake the clean up, or placing signs around the debris to warn people.
Personal Protective Equipment

When cleaning up broken glass, the person or people undertaking the task should wear appropriate Personal Protective Equipment (PPE). The nature of the PPE will vary depending on the nature of the risk. Examples of PPE include:

  • covered shoes

  • protective eyewear

  • puncture proof gloves (in the event it is necessary to handle the glass).

Clean Up Procedure Where possible a dustpan and brush should be used to clean up broken glass. If it is necessary to handle larger pieces of glass puncture proof gloves should be used. Once the glass has been removed, the area should be swept thoroughly (or vacuumed) to remove the chance of small glass particles posing a hazard.
Disposing of Broken Glass

Broken glass will tear through garbage bags or other common waste receptacles. Where possible the broken glass should be stored in heavy cardboard boxes that can be sealed with duct tape or other heavy tape. The boxes of broken glass can be disposed of with other building waste materials.

Broken glass that is contaminated with human body fluids should be handled separately and disposed of as medical waste. (Refer to our Medical and Biological Waste policy).

Staff and Student Responsibilities All staff and students must follow these broken glass safe work practices.
Implementation

This policy is implemented through a combination of:

  • staff training and supervision

  • workplace safety signage

  • effective isolation procedures

  • effective incident notification procedures

  • initiation of corrective actions where necessary.
