Rating a Risk

An overall risk rating is allocated to an identified risk using the following matrix, which indicates the residual risk presented by a combination of various degrees of “likelihood” and “consequence”.

Residual risk is the status of a risk when all risk controls are in place.

The matric used here has been developed exclusively for assessing risk within PlanCheckGo!™. It categorises risk into four levels:

E = Extreme

H = High

M = Moderate

L = Low

Likelihood Consequence
Insignificant Minor Moderate Major Catastrophic
Almost Certain L M H E E
Likely L M H H E
Possible L M M H H
Unlikely L L M M H
Rare L L L M M

Likelihood Definitions

Risk Rating Description of Likelihood
Almost Certain Will almost certainly happen during an excursion.
Likely Many occur during an excursion.
Possible Has a chance of occurring during an excursion.
Unlikely Not likely to occur during an excursion.
Rare Will only occur in exceptional circumstances (would be considered highly unusual).

Consequence Definitions

Risk Rating Description of Consequence
Catastrophic

  • Death or serious permanent disability or harm. Unlikely to be able to return to school

  • Catastrophic reputational harm to the school

  • Intentional or recklessly negligent non-compliance with legal obligations

Major

  • Life-threatening injury or harm requiring lengthy hospitalisation and rehabilitation. More than month out of school

  • Major reputational harm to the school

  • Non-compliance with legal obligations and substantial non-compliance with internal policies

Moderate

  • Injury or harm requiring hospitalisation, however full recovery is expected

  • Moderate reputational harm to the school

  • Non-compliance with internal policies
Minor

  • Minor injury or harm requiring treatment by a doctor, or appropriate medical professional, but not hospitalisation

  • Minor reputation damage to the school

  • Partial non-compliance with internal policies
Insignificant

  • Incident that may require basic first aid and disruption to excursion, however no injury is sustained

  • Little to no reputational harm to the school

  • Little to no non-compliance with internal policies
