Access and Egress Policy and Procedures

Introduction

We are committed to complying with our Work Safe requirements.

Purpose

Clear and safe access (the means of getting in) and egress (the means of getting out) from the workplace are essential to ensuring a safe working environment both within normal working conditions and in emergency situations. This hazard extends to situations where workers are required to access areas that may be considered high risk, such as storage areas and confined spaces.

Scope

This policy and its procedures apply to all staff, volunteers and contractors at the school.

Roles and Responsibilities

All workers are responsible for ensuring that:

  • access and egress areas are in good repair and free from obstacles that may affect their safe usage
  • prompt action is taken to report obstructions where they are identified and to remove any such obstructions, if it is safe to do so.

Policy Statement

Canberra Grammar School is committed to providing safe access and egress within all parts of our workplace.

It is our policy that:

  • workplace access and egress areas are free from obstructions that may restrict movement
  • any such obstructions are identified and removed immediately
  • any access or egress restrictions are adhered to in areas identified as presenting significant hazards
  • regular inspections are undertaken with respect to key access and egress areas to ensure adequate safety standards.

Implementation

This Policy is implemented through a combination of:

  • hazard identification and notification procedures
  • regular workplace safety inspections
  • initiation of corrective actions where necessary
  • staff training

Breach

Where a staff member breaches this Policy Canberra Grammar School may take disciplinary action.

Definitions

Term Definition
Access The means of getting in
Egress The means of getting out
